1d:T10e1,<p>Most creators ship their first product by brute force, writing content, figuring out Gumroad's editor, setting an arbitrary price, and clicking publish. It works once. It doesn't scale.</p>
<p>This toolkit documents the system behind shipping 17 digital products in under 3 months. Not speed-running, but systematizing: every product follows the same pipeline from validation to launch to monitoring.</p>
<p><strong>What you get:</strong></p>
<p>11-chapter guide (7,800+ words, 4 mermaid diagrams) covering:</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 1: The Product Pipeline</strong> The 8-stage pipeline: Ideate, Validate, Build, Package, List, Price, Launch, Monitor. Clear inputs, outputs, and quality gates for each stage.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 2: Validation Before Building</strong> The validation scorecard: 6 weighted criteria with a launch threshold. Where to validate (search volume, forums, competitor analysis). Kill criteria for bad ideas.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 3: Content Creation by Tier</strong> The 6-tier pricing system ($9 / $14 / $19 / $24 / $29 / $39) with word count targets, file counts, and a real shipped Edgeless Lab product named at every tier as the reference point. The "Battle Scars" differentiator, why buyers pay for curation over information.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 4: Packaging and Covers</strong> ZIP structure, quality gates, and automated cover image generation. The template that makes every cover consistent.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 5: Gumroad Listing Optimization</strong> Short description formula, full description structure, cross-sell architecture, and permalink strategy.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 6: Pricing Strategy</strong> Depth-based pricing, the $9 entry product strategy, price anchoring with bundles, and when to raise prices. Includes a "what each price tier actually looks like" breakdown with a real shipped product named for every price point from $9 to $39.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 7: Launch Sequencing</strong> Wave-based launches with narrative threads. Launch day checklist. Post-launch monitoring for the first 4 weeks.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 8: The Content Flywheel</strong> How every product generates blog posts, and every blog post drives product sales. Blog-to-product linking. Email distribution.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 9: Common Mistakes</strong> The 6 mistakes that kill launches, building before validating, pricing based on effort, overloading v1, dead links, no cross-selling, invisible launches.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 10: Shipped Catalog and Measuring Your Own Numbers</strong> The real Edgeless Lab catalog, organized into three launch waves, each with its own narrative thread. Per-product notes on what worked and what did not. Plus the five metrics that actually matter (conversion, refund rate, AOV, bundle lift, cross-sell CTR), their formulas, and exactly where to find each one inside Gumroad analytics.</p>
<p><strong>Chapter 11: Scaling to 50 Products</strong> The repeatable system, pipeline maintenance, when to bundle, and a direct comparison of what makes this different from the free launch advice you can already find (weighted scorecard, narrative wave rule, inter-stage quality gates).</p>
<p><strong>Plus:</strong></p>
<ul><li>Product brief template with validation scorecard</li><li>Gumroad listing copy template (copy-paste ready)</li><li>3 printable checklists: pre-build, pre-package, launch day</li><li>URL verification script (check your listings are live before updating your site)</li><li><strong>Appendix C: The 17 Products</strong>, the complete Edgeless Lab catalog as a ground-truth product index, tiered by price</li><li><strong>Appendix D: Sample Filled-In Product Brief</strong>, the actual brief used to greenlight CLAUDE.md Template Pack, with the validation math shown explicitly</li></ul>
<p><strong>Who this is for:</strong></p>
<ul><li>Developers or creators shipping digital products (guides, templates, code)</li><li>Anyone building a product catalog on Gumroad or similar platforms</li><li>Solo founders who want a repeatable launch process</li></ul>
<p><strong>Who this is NOT for:</strong></p>
<ul><li>If you're building SaaS or physical products, this is for digital downloads only</li><li>If you've shipped 50+ products, you already have a system</li></ul>
