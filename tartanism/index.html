§ 01 What Is a Tartan

Symmetry is the first constraint. A tartan sett is palindromic. The thread sequence reads the same forward and backward, pivoting at each end. This mirror creates the characteristic repeating grid. Without it, the pattern may be decorative, but it is not a tartan.

The second constraint is notation. A tartan is defined by its thread count: R/4 G/24 B/4 K/16. Each letter maps to a color from the traditional palette. Each number is a thread count. The notation is compact, unambiguous, and sufficient to reproduce the pattern on any loom. The notation is the tartan.

The third constraint is registration. A tartan becomes official when entered in the Scottish Register of Tartans, maintained by the National Records of Scotland. Without registration, no matter how precise the sett or how faithful the colors, the pattern is just plaid.

§ 02 The Generator

The algorithm begins with color. From the traditional palette of 48 tartan colors, it selects three to six with sufficient perceptual contrast. No two adjacent stripes share a hue. Thread counts are drawn from constrained ranges and distributed to hit a target total, then the half-sett is mirrored around its pivot threads to guarantee symmetry.

The result is a valid tartan by every structural rule: palindromic sett, named colors, integer thread counts. The only thing it lacks is a clan.

§ 03 The Weaves

The weave structure is the hidden variable. A tartan is not just its thread count. At every intersection, either the warp thread (vertical) or the weft thread (horizontal) passes on top. The pattern of these crossings, the weave, transforms identical threads into different fabrics with different hand, different drape, different visual character.

Six weave structures are defined below. Each uses a tie-up matrix that maps shaft positions to treadle sequences. The 2/2 twill is the canonical tartan weave. The others reveal what happens when you hold the sett constant and change only the loom.

§ 05 From Algorithm to Textile

A generated tartan can be physically woven. The sett notation maps directly to a warp plan. Thread counts become yarn requirements: meters of each color, calculated from the target dimensions and the yarn weight. The weave structure translates to a WIF file, the interchange format understood by computerized looms and desktop weaving software.

Every tartan on this page can travel from algorithm to textile without ambiguity. The studio is where that journey starts.
